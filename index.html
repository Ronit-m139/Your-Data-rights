Your Data Rights

What You Can Do

Data collections is intrusive; however, you are are not powerless. This page shows the steps you can take to protect your privacy, understand your legal rights as an Australian, and also reduce the amount of data platforms collect about you,

EC-11-11 — Communicates to intended audience

01 // Immediate actions you can take

These are concrete steps you can implement today. They won't eliminate data collection entirely, but they will significantly reduce the volume of data platforms can collect and monetise.

01

Review Your Privacy Settings

Most platforms bury privacy controls deep in settings menus. Take 10 minutes to review and restrict data collection permissions on the platforms you use most.

Go to Settings → Privacy on each app you use regularly
Turn off "Personalised Ads" or "Ad Personalisation"
Disable location tracking unless absolutely necessary
Revoke microphone and camera access from apps that don't need them
02

Use Privacy-Focused Tools

Switch to browsers, search engines and messaging apps that don't track you. These tools are free and work just as well as the data-harvesting alternatives.

Browser: Firefox or Brave (instead of Chrome)
Search Engine: DuckDuckGo (instead of Google)
Messaging: Signal (instead of WhatsApp/Messenger)
Install uBlock Origin browser extension to block trackers
03

Limit What You Share

Every post, like and comment adds to your profile. Be deliberate about what you share publicly and what data you volunteer to platforms.

Review who can see your posts (Friends only vs Public)
Avoid "Sign in with Google/Facebook" — create separate accounts
Don't fill out optional profile fields (relationship status, employer, etc.)
Use a separate email for signups (not your main email)
04

Request Your Data

Under Australian law, you have the right to request a copy of the data companies hold about you. This is called a "Data Access Request" and platforms are legally required to comply.

Go to Settings → Privacy → Download Your Data on major platforms
Wait 1-2 weeks for the data file to be prepared
Review what they've collected — you'll be surprised
Use this to inform which platforms you want to continue using
05

Delete Old Accounts

That social media account you haven't used in 3 years? Still collecting and selling your data. Delete accounts you no longer actively use.

Make a list of all accounts you've created but no longer use
Visit each platform's account deletion page (not just deactivation)
Request full data deletion (some platforms retain data otherwise)
Check your email for confirmation of deletion

02 // Your legal rights as an Australian

The Privacy Act 1988 and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) give you certain rights. Knowing these rights helps you hold platforms accountable.

Right to Access

You can request a copy of all personal information an organisation holds about you. They must provide this within 30 days.

Right to Correction

If data about you is inaccurate, you can request it be corrected. The organisation must update their records or explain why they won't.

Right to Complain

If a company mishandles your data, you can lodge a complaint with the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC).

Right to Know Why

Organisations must tell you why they're collecting your data and how it will be used. If they can't explain, they shouldn't collect it.
